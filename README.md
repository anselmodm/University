University

He considers that in a university, there is a staff divided into two groups:
▪ Administrative and Service Personnel (PAS): the administrative staff includes all personnel who perform 
administrative or managerial work of some type of service.

In this case, for simplicity, we will only consider that there are administrative and IT personnel.

▪ Teaching and Research Staff (PDI): personnel dedicated to teaching and research, which includes 
professors and researchers hired for some research project.

All personnel are identified by their name and ID number, and receive a monthly salary that we must 
calculate, in a simplified way, considering the number of working hours per week multiplied by the 
hourly rate of their category and by the number of weeks per month (4). The data for each type of 
personnel are as follows (the quantities are not real):
Administrative: 37 hours/week and a value of 7.5 euros/hour.

IT Technician: 40 hours/week at a rate of 6 euros/hour.
Professors: 37 hours/week at a rate of 8 euros/hour.
Researchers: 35 hours/week at a rate of 7 euros/hour.

Furthermore, professors and PASs (Administrative Support Staff) receive the following additional compensation:
▪ Professors: a salary supplement is added based on what is known as
sexennial periods (positively evaluated 6-year periods) which can be granted up to a maximum of 6 years. 
Specifically, 100 euros per month is added for each recognized sexennial period.

▪ PAS: in a given month, PASs (both administrative and IT) can accumulate overtime. Each overtime hour 
is paid at 6 euros/hour.

In this practical activity you should:

Create a structure of classes and subclasses that represents the university workers. In this structure, 
there must be a superclass common to all
PERSONNEL workers.

▪ Create a UNIVERSITY class with a function that prints to the screen all the university personnel, 
including details for each one such as name, category, and salary.

It should also contain a function that calculates the total amount the university spends on salaries.

To avoid using decimals in monetary amounts, we can work with money in cents of Euro and only convert 
it to euros when displaying it on the screen.
